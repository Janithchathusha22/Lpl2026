🏏 What is the LPL?

The Lanka Premier League is Sri Lanka's pro T20 tournament: 5 teams, fast 20-over matches (~3 hours). Each team has batters, bowlers and all-rounders fighting to win the trophy.

👥 The 4 player types

Batter scores runs. Bowler takes wickets. All-rounder does both. Wicket-keeper catches behind the stumps & also bats. We label every player so you know their job.

⏱️ The 3 phases of an innings

A 20-over innings is split into 3 stages. Players are often great at one stage, not all three:

Powerplay · ov 1-6 Middle · ov 7-15 Death · ov 16-20

Powerplay: attack, field is restricted. Middle: build steadily. Death: all-out hitting at the end.

🔢 How to read the ratings

Every skill is scored 0 to 10 (10 = world-class). We turn these into a single Overall score out of 100 for quick comparison. Higher = better.

📈 Strike rate & average

Strike rate = runs per 100 balls (how fast a batter scores; 150+ is explosive). Average = runs per dismissal (how reliable). Economy = runs a bowler concedes per over (lower is better).

🏟️ Why the stadium matters

Pitches behave differently. Some help fast (pace) bowling, some help spin, some are great for batting. A player's venue rating shows how well they suit each ground.

Cricket dictionary

Every term, in one place
Powerplay (overs 1–6)
Start of the innings; fielding is restricted so batters attack.
Middle overs (7–15)
Batters build the innings and rotate the strike; spinners often bowl here.
Death overs (16–20)
The finish — batters go for maximum runs, bowlers defend.
Strike rate
Runs scored per 100 balls. 150+ means very fast, attacking batting.
Batting average
Runs scored per time out. Higher = more consistent and reliable.
Economy
Runs a bowler gives away per over. Lower is better (under 7 is good in T20).
Wicket
Getting a batter out. Bowlers want wickets; “wkts/match” = expected wickets per game.
All-rounder
A player good at both batting and bowling.
Pace vs spin
Two bowling styles: pace = fast, spin = slow with turn. Pitches favour one or the other.
Venue / pitch
The ground and its surface. It changes how easy batting or bowling is.
Toss
A coin flip before the match; the winner chooses to bat or bowl first — a real tactical edge.
Bat first vs Chase
Bat first = set a target. Chase = bowl first, then try to beat the target. Conditions decide which is smarter.
Dew
Night moisture that makes the ball wet and slippery, so bowling gets harder — chasing teams often benefit.
Safe score / target
A first-innings total that's usually enough to win on that pitch.
Monte-Carlo simulation
Playing the tournament thousands of times with the model to see how often each outcome happens.
Win probability
The model's estimate of each team's chance of winning a single match, shown as a split bar.
